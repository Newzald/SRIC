The Socially Responsible Investment Committee of Bard College

SRIC writes Shareholder Resolutions that demand business practices reflect our values.

Most universities in the United States have large endowments that they invest in stocks. Where these funds are large enough, investment and divestment — as acts in themselves — can influence the decisions of companies. Bard's funds are not large enough to influence companies in this way, so we use a different approach to engage with companies. Bard's Socially Responsible Investment Committee writes Shareholder Resolutions that demand business practices reflect the values of the Bard student body. The resolutions offer alternatives that we deem both more ethical as well as more profitable. We welcome Bard students to propose new companies we can invest in and whom we could engage in a shareholder resolution campaign. Divestment or passive investment proposals will rarely be considered.

A shareholder resolution is a proposal submitted by shareholders for a change in the usual practices of the company. Shareholder resolutions usually focus on issues of corporate governance, like executive compensation or corporate social responsibility issues. Although almost all shareholder resolutions are nonbinding, corporates tend to follow through on the results of a resolution so as to avoid issues with reputational risk. Therefore, shareholder resolutions offer an effective way to increase corporate social responsibility.

Current Project

Urban Outfitters, the popular multinational retailer based in Philadelphia, PA, has gained notoriety from the sourcing of its cotton.

Urban Outfitters cotton is sourced from plantations in Uzbekistan, where plantation owners use slave labor to keep down the costs of production. The SRIC will write a shareholder resolution urging Urban Outfitters to source its cotton more ethically. This matter is especially important to Bardians because it is a popular brand on campus.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is SRIC different from the Fiscal Committee?
The SRIC is a branch of the Fiscal Committee. It has members that are elected directly to the committee and are independent of Fiscal Committee.
Do you guys make Bard money?
Yes. Some years are better than others.
Bard has professionals that manage their funds, right? Do you guys cooperate with them?
Yes. The social choice fund is the portion of Bard funds with which the SRIC can engage. The money is managed in a hedge fund. We are not in direct contact with the hedge fund managers, but we work with Taun Toay and James Brudvig, who communicate our wishes and concerns.
